Zoo Rules

No flash photography: The bright light can startle or scare animals.

Keep a safe distance: Stay behind barriers and on designated paths. Animals are not tame and can be unpredictable.

o not feed the animals: They have specific diets, and human food can be harmful or even fatal.

Do not disturb the animals: Avoid shouting, banging on glass, or making sudden movements that could stress them.

Stay on paths and obey barriers: Never climb on, over, or under barriers, as they are there to keep you safe.

Follow staff instructions: Zoo staff are trained to ensure the safety of both visitors and animals. Listen to their directions.

Proper disposal: Use the bins provided to dispose of trash properly.

Our Mission at the Zoo

A look into our history, values, and dedication to wildlife conservation.

Our Story

Founded in **1985** by renowned conservationist Dr. Jane Miller, our Zoo was built on the principle that seeing is saving. We started with just a handful of rescued native species and have grown into a leading facility, dedicated to creating immersive, natural habitats for over 200 species from around the globe.

Every ticket purchased goes directly back into animal care, habitat maintenance, and global conservation programs. We're more than just a place to visit‚Äîwe're a dedicated center for preservation.

Conservation & Education

Our commitment extends far beyond our gates. We actively participate in **Species Survival Plans (SSP)** for critically endangered animals, including our Amur Tigers and Eastern Gorillas. Through research partnerships and field programs, we work tirelessly to protect wildlife in its natural habitat.

We believe **education is key to conservation**. Our interactive exhibits, daily keeper talks, and youth camps aim to inspire the next generation of wildlife advocates. Come learn, explore, and become a part of the solution!
